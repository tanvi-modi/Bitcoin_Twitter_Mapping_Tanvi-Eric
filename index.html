Bitcoin Popularity in the USA

Is bitcoin a new gold rush? Bitcoin and Cryptocurrencies have gained momentum in the past decade. During thanksgiving in 2017, most dinner with family were talking about the new tech fad, "the bitcoin" and how this is the next big thing. Just after that evryone started investing in bitcoin heavily, only to lose their money by more than 5 times. It fell from 17k to 3k in the next 2 years. Everyone started bashing the concept and the bitcoin miners and enthusiasts were trolled in twitter almost everyday.


Until 2020 happened, the crypto market entered bull run again, it shattered the financial market and all the naysayers. Bitcoin became as valuable as the gold market with trillions of market cap. I have shown how twitter users are talking about it in the last week with their geo location. The tweets were collected using the Twitter API with the #bitcoin hashtag having geo information. They were then aggregated based on their location using a frequency count dictionary. These locations are plotted in the US map with their count as circles, with radius as their count origination from that specific city. This visualization shows which cities in the US are actively discussion about bitcoin.

Weekly Bitcoin Prices in USD from September 2020 – March 2021

Back in 2010, there were miners who used 5 bitcoins to buy pizzas. it costed less than a dollar and almost none talked or knew about it. 10 years later, owning 5 to 10 bitcoins would give you generational wealth. Is bitcoin a new gold rush? Yes, we believe so. The prices have started climbing up to all the high of 20k by late 2020. All the financial pundits who wrote off crypto as a scam, were regretting not investing in bitcoin and not being the initial adapters. Now 1 bitcoin costs 60k as of March 2021. This shows how the bitcoin popularity soared in the above graph, where we have shown the price fluctuations starting from September 2020 till March 2021.
